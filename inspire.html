Special Cases
Unique requirements to know
These exams have requirements that most candidates miss
📋

NEET 2026: Webcam + Aadhaar

NEET UG 2026 introduced live photo capture via webcam during online application. This live photo is matched against your Aadhaar. A pre-clicked photo will no longer work for initial registration.

New 2026
✍️

UPSC: Triple Signature Image

From 2026, UPSC CSE requires candidates to sign three times vertically on a single white sheet, scan all three as one image, and upload it. Single signature images are now insufficient.

New 2026
📅

UPSC: Name + Date on Photo

UPSC is the only major exam where the photo must have the candidate's name and date printed on it. This is printed text on the photo itself, not handwritten. ~4.2% of UPSC applications are rejected for this alone.

UPSC Unique
🖼️

NEET: Two Different Photo Sizes

NEET requires both a 2.5×3.5 inch passport photo AND a 4×6 inch postcard photo — with the candidate's name and date printed on both. No other major exam requires a postcard-sized photo.

NEET Unique
📐

India Post GDS: Ratio Math Check

The GDS portal calculates the aspect ratio of uploaded images and throws a hard error if it's even slightly off. Photo must be exactly 4:5 (±10%) and signature 5:2 (±5%). Most photo tools fail this check.

Portal Enforced
👆

Banking Exams: Thumb + Declaration

IBPS exams require 4 separate uploads: photo, signature, left thumb impression (240×240 px in blue ink), and a handwritten declaration. SBI PO/Clerk also needs the thumb impression. Each must meet separate specs.
